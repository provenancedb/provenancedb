# Why ProvenanceDB Exists

## A Manifesto for Trusted Knowledge Systems

Modern databases are among the greatest achievements in computing.

Relational databases such as PostgreSQL transformed how organizations store and manage structured information. Document databases such as MongoDB provided flexibility for modern applications. Vector databases emerged to support similarity search and large-scale AI systems.

These technologies solved important problems.

They were not designed to solve the problem we face today.

The challenge of the next decade is not merely storing information.

The challenge is determining whether information can be trusted.

Every day, organizations collect, generate, transform, and distribute vast amounts of data. Artificial intelligence systems consume this information at unprecedented scale. Knowledge workers increasingly depend on AI-generated outputs to make decisions, conduct research, evaluate evidence, and discover new insights.

Yet modern databases largely answer only one question:

"What data do we have?"

They rarely answer the questions that increasingly matter:

"Where did this data come from?"

"Why should we trust it?"

"When was it last verified?"

"What evidence supports it?"

"How has it changed over time?"

"Can an AI system explain why it used this information?"

These questions are no longer optional.

They are becoming fundamental requirements for trustworthy computing.

ProvenanceDB exists because information without provenance is increasingly insufficient.

A database record should not simply store a value.

It should store the story behind that value.

Every fact should be connected to its source.

Every claim should be connected to supporting evidence.

Every change should be traceable.

Every confidence score should be explainable.

Every AI-generated conclusion should be auditable.

In ProvenanceDB, provenance is not an afterthought.

It is a first-class primitive.

Just as relational databases introduced tables and relationships as core concepts, ProvenanceDB introduces lineage, evidence, attribution, verification, confidence, and trust as native building blocks of the data model.

A record is no longer just data.

A record becomes knowledge.

Knowledge is more than information.

Knowledge includes context.

Knowledge includes evidence.

Knowledge includes uncertainty.

Knowledge includes history.

The rise of artificial intelligence makes this shift even more important.

Most AI systems today are built on a fragile foundation. Models retrieve information from databases, documents, APIs, and vector stores. They generate outputs that appear authoritative while often obscuring the origins of the underlying information.

This creates a growing trust gap.

Users want answers.

Organizations need explanations.

Regulators increasingly demand accountability.

Researchers require reproducibility.

ProvenanceDB is designed to close that gap.

Rather than treating provenance as metadata stored somewhere else, ProvenanceDB treats provenance as part of the data itself.

The goal is not to replace PostgreSQL, MongoDB, or existing vector databases.

Those systems remain exceptional at what they were designed to do.

ProvenanceDB serves a different purpose.

It is designed for environments where trust matters as much as storage.

It is designed for research repositories.

It is designed for curated datasets.

It is designed for knowledge graphs.

It is designed for digital archives.

It is designed for AI memory systems.

It is designed for evidence-based decision making.

It is designed for organizations that need to know not only what they know, but why they know it.

The future of information systems will not be defined solely by scale, speed, or storage efficiency.

It will be defined by trust.

The next generation of databases must help humans and AI systems understand the origins, quality, reliability, and evolution of information.

They must preserve not only facts, but the context that gives those facts meaning.

That is why ProvenanceDB exists.

To build a future where every fact can be traced, every source can be verified, and every decision can be grounded in evidence.

Trust every fact.

Trace every source.
